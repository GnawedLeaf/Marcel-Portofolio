Revamping the Interface
of InPlace
A group project to improve NTU's Internship finding platform as part of DV2008 Interface Design Module
Context
Our team consisted of 5 people: Ryan, Kai, Zi Qi, Xinyi and Me

At the start of the semester, we were tasked to choose an interface and improve it based on the lessons taught throughout the 13 weeks and present a final high fidelity prototype.

We chose InPlace, an internship-finding platform that every NTU student doing a credit bearing internship had to use. Students had to use this platform to find, apply and accept internships however, the interface is far from user friendly.

So we started work right away.

In this project I was involved in organising the team, conducting user research and designing prototypes
The Problem
Home Page
Filter Page
Search Page
Profile Page
It was painful to look at, but unfortunately we had to ask 16 more people to look at it again during user interviews. We also sent out surveys and recieved 43 responses.
Here is some of what we gathered:
Interview Findings
6 first time users of InPlace
UI is unsightly: Too wordy - information overload and repeated information is annoying which makes the listings look suspicious.

Filtering system too broad: People do not know what is the difference between keyword and search filter.

All information required are present: Despite poor organisation of information, all the important information is there.
10 users of InPlace
Information not well organised: Inconsistent information, no company logo, information is too wordy

Filtering system does not work: Clicking on some parts of a filter does not actually apply them

Convenient to submit CV: Attach and submit, no redirecting
Survey Findings
Top 3 things users look for when searching for an internship:
1. Job Scope
2. Company
3. Salary
Filters users would like to have on an internship searching platform:
1. Location
2. Industry of Company
3. Avaliable Status of Internship
4. Salary
Once we understood the pain points of the user experience, we laid out the problems we wanted to solve and set some basic design rules.
Then everyone in the group seperated to start working on their own prototype in order to prevent any influence on each other's creativity.
My Prototype
Filter and search bar fixed on top of screen
Instead of Inplace's filter which opens a new page with confusing filters, this filter is displayed with the top things users want to see.


Search results are spilt into boxes
After some research into other job searching platforms, I found this system of displaying search results was the most easy to use and intuitive.


Important details displayed in small box
Based off the research we conducted, I chose to display internship title, scope, salary, location and duration, as these were what users wanted to see.


Full internship details on other half of screen
Users are not directed to a new page for details and can quickly click on another job if they do not like the current job they are looking at, unlike Inplace which opens up a new page.


Tab System
Users would be able to view a few jobs they're interested in at once and do a quick comparison.

We got back together, shared our ideas, mixed and matched different features from each prototype and ended up with 3 low fidelity prototypes to be tested.
User Testing
We pitted the prototypes agasint each other by getting users to explore each prototype while voicing their thought process out loud.

Users were then asked various questions on the ease of use and the functionality of each prototype. We recorded the interviews with screen recordings and transcripts.

The interview data was analysed and a list of key findings of each prototype was compiled on Figma.
Prototype 1 (Marcel & Xinyi)
Liked the tab functionality.

Liked the company review function.

Prefers the format where you can see other jobs and quickly compare other jobs.

Wanted date for reviews and review ratings should stick to the same format (either stars or positive/negative).
Prototype 2 (Ryan & Ziqi)
Liked timeline feature on the home page.

Liked quick upload and document feature.

Confused between the "shortlisted" and "favourites" tab.
Prototype 3 (Kai)
Liked the compare function where you can select 3 jobs at once and compare them side by side although some were confused on how to use it at first.

Liked the location filter but needed to add "central" as a location.

Did not want the job details to open up to another page.
The team looked through the feedback, extracting out the features we wanted to keep and the threw out the ones we didn't.

Then Kai, Ryan and I started working on the final prototype.
Final Prototype
Our new and improved InPlace
Home Page and Profile
Home Page
Timeline to show the different dates of application openings and gives students a good way to plan their time.

Notifications and To-dos are clear in the front and easy for user access.
Profile
Allows users to upload their internship files and will be auto loaded when applying.

Allows for multiple uploads as well.
Search Results Page
Search Results displayed in small boxes
Cleaned up the box to reduce clutter of information and only displayed what was important to give more readability.

Company logo was added to give a face to the internship and also giving the space more colour.

Area tags and applied tags were added and coloured so users would be able to see them quickly .

Date of internship posting was added so users could make a deicison on if it was relevant to them.

Bookmark and Comparison functions were added as icons in the box so users could access it easily.
Details Page
Picture banner was added to give more context to the company and give off a the vibe of the company.

Responsibilites and Requirements were at the top of the details page and organised in point form for more clarity.

Extra feature of number of applicants was also added.

Embeded Map added to show the location of nearby amenities and area.

Applied button put at the bottom of the page so users can apply once they have made up their mind.
Filter Bar
Filters based on research on what users wanted to see the most.

Search bar added to help with quick searches.

Sorting function so users can easily sort jobs.


Tab System
Kept due to popularity among users and allows for easy and quick comparing between jobs.
Bookmark and Comparison Pages
Bookmark
Users can save jobs by clicking the bookmark icon and see them all listed in this section.
Comparing Feature
Users can press the compare icon and it will turn blue and open a new tab. Users can add up to 3 jobs to compare between them.

Opens up a new page as this allows them to see the information in a big screen and do their comparison and see which job they want to apply for.

Apply and More Details buttons are at the bottom of the page so users can apply directly instead of having to return to the previous page.
Applied Page
Timeline
Users would be able to see the progress of their application in the application tab and get updated so they will know if their application was sent and sucessful or not
Offer Acceptance Flow
Accept or Decline
Kept the interface simple and straightforward as well as a confirmation pop up in case user the user did not mean to accept or decline the offer
